Eligibility Criteria for MBBS in Uzbekistan (for Indian Students)

Academic Qualification

  • Must have completed 12th grade with at least 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB)
  • Must have studied English as a compulsory subject

NEET Qualification

  • Must have cleared the NEET exam, as mandated by the National Medical Commission (NMC) for studying MBBS abroad
  • NEET qualification is essential for appearing in the Foreign Medical Graduates Examination (FMGE) in India after completing MBBS

Age Requirement

  • Must be 17 years or older on or before December 31st of the year of admission
  • No upper age limit for MBBS in Uzbekistan

Additional Requirements

  • Valid passport with minimum 18 months validity
  • Medical fitness certificate
  • No criminal record
  • HIV test report (may be required for visa)

Language Requirements

Is it necessary to learn Uzbek to study MBBS in Uzbekistan?

While the medium of instruction at most medical universities in Uzbekistan is English, students may encounter language challenges outside the classroom. The official language is Uzbek, and many locals have limited English proficiency. This can affect daily communication and community integration.

Key Points:
  1. Medium of Instruction: English (for international students)
  2. Local Language: Uzbek (Cyrillic script)
  3. Language Support:
    1. Most universities offer Uzbek/Russian language classes for international students
    2. Basic knowledge of Uzbek/Russian is helpful for daily life and clinical rotations
    3. English-speaking staff available at international student offices

Safety for Indian Students

Is it safe for Indian students to study MBBS in Uzbekistan?

Uzbekistan is considered a safe country for Indian students, with low crime rates and a friendly, supportive local community. A vibrant Indian diaspora also offers additional support and a sense of familiarity.

Safety Features:
  1. Low Crime Rate: Uzbekistan has a relatively low crime rate compared to many other countries
  2. Friendly Locals: Uzbeks are known for their hospitality towards international students
  3. Indian Community: Significant Indian student population in major cities
  4. University Support: Dedicated international student offices provide assistance and support
  5. 24/7 Security: Most university campuses have round-the-clock security
  6. Emergency Services: Accessible emergency numbers for police, medical, and other services
Tips for Students:
  1. Register with the Indian Embassy upon arrival
  2. Keep important documents safe and make copies
  3. Be aware of local laws and customs
  4. Use university transportation or reliable taxi services
  5. Stay connected with the Indian student community
